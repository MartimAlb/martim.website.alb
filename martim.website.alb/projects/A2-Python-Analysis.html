Number of Average Rentals Per Temperature¶

The above line graph shows that the numbers of bike rentals is higher as the temperatures increases. On the other hand, the number of bike rentals considerably decreases when tempearture are bellow 0ºC.

The average rentals gradually increases as temperature goes into the 15–25°C range, this appears to be the optimal outdoor riding temperature zone. This might mean that users prefer to rent a bike in warmer weather for leisure and bike commuting.

However, above 30°C, the pattern is much less regular, which is suggestive that very hot weather discourages users to rent bikes.

Overall, moderate weather has a positive effect on bike rental demand, and thus provides useful information for marketing and operational decisions.

Number of Average Rentals Per Amount of Rainfall (mm)¶

The above line graph displays the number of average bike rentals per mm of rainfall. Overall, when rainfall increases, theres is a sharp fall in the number of rentals.

This should come as little surprise, since biking in the rain is usually unpleasant and possibly dangerous, especially for leisure riders or commuter cyclists who lack wet weather gear.

This insight not only emphasizes the role of weather in user behavior, but also points to an important mechanism between weather and crime — both relate to social interactions. This information could be used by the company to limit the number of available bikes on rainy days, or even simply to change the strategy of marketing depending on the weather prediction.

Even light rainfall appears to negatively impact rental volume, which suggests that users are highly sensitive to changes in weather.

Number of Average Rentals Per Humidity Percentage¶

The above graph represents the number of average bike rentals per humidity percentage . It can be seen that when humidity ranges from 40% to 60%, rental numbers are higher.

However, particularly at around 70% or 80% plus, the average number of rentals starts to decrease. This may be due to the discomfort associated with sticky or muggy weather, meaning that biking becomes unappealing — especially for casual or non-commuter users.

This pattern indicates that extreme humidity is a limiting factor for outdoor activity. The company could use this insight to adjust expectations and operations during high-humidity days, or explore strategies like offering discounts to encourage usage during these times.

Why Are the Majority of Users Renting Bicycles¶

According to the distribution of rental times, the majority of users rented their bikes during the normal commute times, mainly between 8 AM and 5 to 6 PM. These spikes appear to indicate that thousands of users are using bikes for daily mobility, probably going to work or school.

Similar to some global urban bike-share trends, this behavior reflects convenience, cost savings, and a means of escaping traffic, all of which make biking an attractive alternative here. We can see further evidence of that conclusion in the way rentals are plummeting during hours that are not between commutes.

In general, most users are probably busy people using bikes as a functional commuting method through the city in peak hours.

Ideal Weather Conditions for High Bike Rental Demand¶

Demand for bike rental is hugely affected by the weather conditions. During moderate temperatures (15°C-25°C) and little to no rain, rentals are at the highest level. The mild waether helps biking to be more pleasant and safe.

Both rentals are adversely affected by rainfall and high humidity. Minor rainfall also seems to lead to a significant decrease in observed usage too, along with extremely humid days (>80%). Perhaps for safety or comfort reasons, these factors seem to deter casual and commuter bikers.

The best days to rent are clear, relatively warm and with low humidity.

With this insights the company is able to plan marketing, maintenance, and the availability of the fleet around these conditions.

Strategic Recommendations Based on the Analysis¶

The company should increase bike availability and operational resources during the peak of commute times, mainly on weekday monrnings and evenings. This strategy matches with the user behavior patterns displayed in the graphs.

Moreover,since there is a correlation between low demadn and bad weather, the company could decrease the number of bikes deployed on bad weather days or offer discounts during such days to boost usage. Predictive weather-based planning could decrease costs and unnecessary bike repositioning or maintenance.

Working professionals or people who do not own a car or a motorbike should be targeted in marketing campaings during mild weather months, as well as promotions to increase costumer satisfaction.

Conclusion¶

This analyzes provided important and valuable insights regarding how time and weather conditions influence the behaviour of users in bike rental. Additionally, it can be seen from the analyzes that the majority of rental occurs during weekday commuitng hours. This demonstrates that several users are relying on bikes as their main transportation mean.

Moreover, weather also has a crucial role in user behavior. Most rental happen on warm and dry days with low humidity. On the other hand, rentals are considerably lower when it rains or when theres is extreme humidty. Thus, these patterns suggest that confort and safety are crucial factors when it comes to rental decisions.

Identifying and interpreting these patterns will allow the company to increase the optimization of its operations, improve user satisfaction and align business strategies with real user needs.
